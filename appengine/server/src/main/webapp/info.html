Backup your Android photos


Do you have a safe copy of your photos and videos in case your phone breaks or get stolen?
If not, this webpage explains how you can make sure you will never loose your valuable life memories.

There are two popular backup solutions for Android: Google+ Instant Upload and Dropbox Camera Upload. They are fine solutions but have one major drawback: your backups are stored in low quality or you have to pay a monthly subscription if you want to backup your photos and videos in the original high quality format.

Recently, Flickr changed their photos/videos hosting offer. They now offer an astounding terabyte of storage for FREE! This storage is so huge that to fill it up with photos you will have to take 100 pictures a day for the next 20 years! And Flickr, which is owned by Yahoo, has been running since 2004 so you can rest assured your photos are in good hands.

Flickr does not yet offer a simple backup solution for your photos and videos though. Their Android app, for instance, does not allow to upload folders of photos and most importantly does automatically backup your photos everyday.

That is why I created Flickr Uploader. It is a simple yet powerful app that will let you backup your photos and videos to Flickr. You just have to set it up once and every time you take a picture, it will make sure a copy is uploaded to Flickr.
